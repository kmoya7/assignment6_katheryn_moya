Figure 1. Mono Lake levels (in feet above sea level) from 1850 - 2017. Major events have had large impacts on lake levels, including the diversion of water from the tributary streams of Mono Lake by the Los Angeles Department of Water & Power (DWP) in 1941. This resulted in a steep decline in lake levels over the next four decades, which exposed a land bridge 6,377 feet above sea level (shown in red). This land bridge formation decreased local water bird populations as previously protected nesting sites became available to predators. A notable shift in this trend occurred in 1984 through the first of eight lawsuits in the 1980s that challeged DWP using the California Department of Fish & Game codes. These successful challenges led to water flowing into Mono Lake once again for the first time since 1941.
